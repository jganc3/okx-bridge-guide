## How Cross-Chain Bridging Actually Works

To understand why OKX Bridge is built the way it is, it helps to understand what bridging actually does under the hood.

When you bridge a token from, say, Ethereum to BNB Chain, the bridge's smart contract locks your original tokens on the Ethereum network. Once that lock is verified, an equivalent amount of tokens is minted or released on the BNB Chain side. You're not literally sending your ETH across chains — you're locking it in a vault on one side and unlocking a corresponding amount on the other.

This sounds simple, but the execution is where things get complicated. The smart contracts managing the lock-and-mint process are attractive targets for hackers. Bridge exploits have been some of the largest in crypto history — the Wormhole hack alone resulted in $320 million in losses. OKX Bridge mitigates this risk through aggregated routing (spreading risk across multiple audited protocols), continuous code review, and that insurance fund we mentioned.

It's also worth distinguishing bridging from regular swapping. An on-chain swap on Uniswap trades one token for another within the same network. A bridge moves your assets between entirely different blockchains. You use a swap when you want to trade ETH for USDC on Ethereum. You use a bridge when you want to move that USDC from Ethereum to Solana.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid When Bridging

Bridging is generally safe when done correctly, but there are a handful of errors that catch users off guard:

- **Wallet network mismatch**: Your wallet's active network must match your selected source chain. If you're bridging from Arbitrum but your wallet is connected to Ethereum, the transaction fails. Always verify before confirming.
- **Wrong receiving address**: When bridging to a new chain, the receiving address is automatically generated for the target network. Don't manually override this unless you absolutely know what you're doing — sending to a wrong-network address can result in permanent loss.
- **Insufficient gas on destination chain**: You need gas on the target chain to actually use the tokens once they arrive. If you bridge ETH to BNB Chain but have no BNB to pay for transactions there, your bridged assets are stuck until you fund gas somehow.
- **Ignoring fee estimates**: OKX Bridge shows all fees upfront. If a route looks unusually expensive, check whether network congestion is driving up gas costs — sometimes waiting a few hours saves significant money on large transfers.
- **Falling for phishing sites**: Only access the bridge through the official OKX Web3 domain. Bookmark it. Fake bridge interfaces are a common attack vector.
